What is Quantum Field Theory?

As the name suggests, it is a theory that attempts to explain the world of quantum physics using mathematical objects called fields.

  • Theory - A set of ideas that are tested for robustness against experimental observations. You cannot prove a theory to be correct, you can only prove a theory to not be wrong by passing all the tests!
  • Quantum - A discrete entity (not continuous) in the language of mathematics. For example, the electronic configuration of atoms are described with discretely-spaced energy levels. An electron jumps from one energy level to another by releasing or absorbing a fixed amount of energy. The discrete values of these energy levels are determined by solving quantum mechanical equations in three dimensions, see spherical harmonics for more details. There is a misconception that quantum refers to the "small" world, but it actually refers to the "discrete" world.
  • Fields - A field is a mathematical object that represents a fundamental or composite particle. A field is defined by its symmetries, i.e. which mathematical operations return the object to its original state (this is called invariance).
