Academic background

I began my higher education studies at Royal Holloway, University of London in September 2009. The course I underook was an MSci Physics, also known as an "Integrated Masters" (first 2.5 years on a Bachelors programme and last 1.5 years on a Masters programme). During this time I picked up a real passion for wanting to understand the structure of the world in every aspect. Fortunately, I succeeded in obtaining a First Class Honours degree and found a position as a PhD student in Theoretical Particle Physics at the Universtiy of Sussex.

My PhD goal was to investigate the theoretical conditions underlying a cosmological event in the early universe, whereby all the Standard Model particles obtain a non-zero rest mass. This event is the Electroweak Phase Transition, which embodies the famous Higgs Mechanism. My doctoral studies resulted in a publication that discovered a link between a zero temperature quantity of any model and the strength of the phase transition.

IT industry background
